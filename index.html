The Legacy of Mihaly Huszka (1933–2022)

Born in Hungary in 1933, Mihaly Huszka rose to become a two-time Olympian, two-time World & European silver medalist, and five-time Hungarian National Champion. In 1965, at the peak of his career, he defected from communist Hungary—risking everything for freedom.

After arriving in America in 1967, Huszka dedicated his life to coaching and competing well into his 80s. He claimed 22 World Masters Championships, set over 100 world records, and was named the "Grand Master Weightlifter of the Century."

Today, we carry forward his Hungarian training methods—technical precision, explosive power, and the belief that strength is for life.
